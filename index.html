About the Album

As We Once Were is the third studio album from twice Welsh Music Prize nominated artist and producer The Anchoress (aka Catherine Anne Davies) — the long-awaited follow-up to her UK Top 40 album The Art Of Losing.

Created at Black Lodge Studios with access to Pete Townshend's collection of vintage analogue synthesisers, the album is an epic 14-track, widescreen exploration of the nature of time and transformation. Against the backdrop of the birth of her first child and the discovery of her grandmother's voice on a long-lost cassette tape, Davies asks: What can we build out of the past? Are we destined to be held down by it? Can we ever return to these past versions of ourselves?

About The Anchoress

Catherine Anne Davies

The Anchoress is Catherine Anne Davies — Welsh multi-instrumentalist, self-producing artist, and one of the most distinctive voices in British music. She writes, produces, and plays everything herself: piano, guitar, flute, mellotron, synthesisers, and more. Her second album The Art of Losing reached the UK Top 40 and was named one of Elton John's favourite records of the year. She has collaborated with James Dean Bradfield of the Manic Street Preachers, performed at Robert Smith's Meltdown Festival, and been championed by BBC Radio 6 Music, NME, PROG, Sunday Times, Line of Best Fit, and more.
