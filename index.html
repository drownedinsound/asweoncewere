About The Anchoress

Catherine Anne Davies

The Anchoress is Catherine Anne Davies — Welsh multi-instrumentalist, self-producing artist, and one of the most distinctive voices in British music. She writes, produces, and plays everything herself: piano, guitar, flute, mellotron, synthesisers, and more. Her second album The Art of Losing reached the UK Top 40, earned a Metacritic score of 83, and was named PROG Album of the Year. She has collaborated with James Dean Bradfield of the Manic Street Preachers and been championed by BBC Radio 6 Music, NME, PROG, Sunday Times, Line of Best Fit, and more. Recorded at Black Lodge Studios with exclusive access to Pete Townshend from The Who's personal collection of vintage analogue synthesisers, it is her most sonically ambitious work to date.

About the Album

As We Once Were is the third studio album from twice Welsh Music Prize nominated artist and producer The Anchoress (aka Catherine Anne Davies) — the long-awaited follow-up to her UK Top 40 album The Art Of Losing.

Created at Black Lodge Studios with access to Pete Townshend's collection of vintage analogue synthesisers, the album is an epic 14-track, widescreen exploration of the nature of time and transformation. Against the backdrop of the birth of her first child and the discovery of her grandmother's voice on a long-lost cassette tape, Davies asks: What can we build out of the past? Are we destined to be held down by it? Can we ever return to these past versions of ourselves?

New Podcast — Coming Soon

The Milk & The Music

Five years ago, I became a mother. What I didn't expect was how profoundly it would challenge everything I thought I understood about my work, my identity, and my place in this industry.

So I started having conversations — honest ones, with women across music who are navigating the same impossible puzzle. Not the "having it all" version. The real, complicated truth about creativity, guilt, childcare costs, and what it reveals about an industry when it makes parenting nearly impossible.

Featuring conversations with Gazelle Twin, Lau.ra, Olga Fitzroy, and Charlotte Hatherley.

About this podcast expand_more

I started talking to other women in music who are mothers, and I realised we were all navigating versions of the same impossible puzzle — that peculiar mix of grief and guilt and determination. We were all asking: how do we keep making work that matters while also raising children? And more importantly: why does it still feel like we're doing this despite the industry, rather than with its support?

This series isn't about celebrating how we've all managed to juggle everything beautifully. It's about naming what's hard, what's broken, and what needs to change. About the creative process when your time, your body, and your sense of self are fundamentally altered. About money, and how childcare costs interact with freelance income in ways that make some opportunities impossible. About whether the music community shows up for parents — or quietly forgets about them.

Gazelle Twin — Elizabeth Bernholz

Whose uncompromising electronic music and visceral live performances have made her one of the most vital artists working today.

Lau.ra — Laura Bettinson

Award-winning electronic music producer and the first woman to win the Music Producers Guild award for Self-Producing Artist. One half of Ultraísta with Radiohead producer Nigel Godrich.

Olga Fitzroy

Renowned recording and mixing engineer whose credits span The Crown, Coldplay, and Dua Lipa. Has campaigned tirelessly for shared parental leave and fair pay for freelance parents.

Charlotte Hatherley

Whose work with Ash and as a solo artist has spanned decades.

These are women who've refused to disappear, who've kept making important work — and who've got things to say about what needs to change.
